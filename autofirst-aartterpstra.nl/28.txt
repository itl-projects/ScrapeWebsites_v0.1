You drive it most of the year. They perform best at temperatures above 7 degrees, where they provide optimal grip and comfort. Thanks to the harder rubber compound, they have a lower rolling resistance. This in turn has a favorable effect on fuel consumption. In addition, the hard material makes a summer tire more wear-resistant compared to the more elastic winter tire. What are the best summer tires for me? The choice of summer tire depends on your driving style, type of car and the available budget. An A-quality tire performs well in all areas. They have excellent properties on both dry and wet surfaces. They are silent, do not consume too much and score well for wear resistance and durability at high speeds.
