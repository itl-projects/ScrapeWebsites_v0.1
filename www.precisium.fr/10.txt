It is the interface between: the crankshaft the injection pump the water pump (more and more often) the camshaft (s) which control the intake and exhaust valves. It is responsible for synchronizing the movement between the valves and the pistons. There are two main types of distribution: Most often it is a belt, And some engines use a chain. HOW DO YOU KNOW THAT YOU MUST REPAIR YOUR TIMING BELT? The timing belt is very stressed and especially during each start-up. So take great care because if it breaks, it causes a lot of damage to your engine. What are the signs that can alert you? - a noise of friction or screeching in your engine. - a leak of liquid (oil, petrol, coolant). WHAT TO DO? ASK FOR ADVICE FROM A PRECISIUM PROFESSIONAL TO CHECK YOUR VEHICLE WHEN IS CHANGING THE TIMING BELT NORMALLY RECOMMENDED? You must follow the instructions in the vehicle manufacturer's maintenance booklet. When should the first change be made? After 5 years or between 70,000 and 100,000 km. And then ? Regular verification should be done. Then you must make a change every 120,000 km or every 5 years for vehicles before 2010 and every 160,000 km or 6 years for vehicles after 2010.
