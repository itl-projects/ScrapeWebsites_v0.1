From the moment you step on the brake pedal, the brake fluid transmits the braking force from the master cylinder to the wheels to slow or brake the vehicle. The pressure then activates the caliper which clamps the brake pads against the disc (disc brake) or spreads the jaws which are supported on the drum (drum brake) to brake the rotation of the wheels. Any anomaly in the braking system (pads, discs, calipers, condition of the hoses, etc.) noted at the technical control leads to a compulsory counter-visit. The braking system is very busy and especially in urban areas. You must therefore take great care of it because in the event of a malfunction, it is your safety and that of other users, because the braking distances increase considerably or even become non-existent. The signs that can alert you? pedal (stiffer or softer). A change in the behavior of your vehicle with a vibration going up to the level of the pedal. Vehicles seem to be grazing or vibrating when braking. A decrease in brake fluid. The brake light comes on. A hissing sound when braking. A warm smell when braking. WHAT TO DO? Seek the advice of a Precisium professional to check your vehicle! WHEN IS CHANGE NORMALLY RECOMMENDED? You must follow the instructions in the vehicle manufacturer's maintenance book. The brake pads must be checked every 20,000 km or every 2 years. Brake fluid must be replaced every 30,000 km or every 2 years. Each brake disc or brake drum must be checked every 50,000 km. REMEMBER TO CHECK: Your brake system Your brake fluid level before each vacation or long trip.
