If you are traveling with your family or as a couple, don't ruin your vacation because of a problem with your vehicle! At Précisium we help you prepare your vehicle before your summer vacation. Here is a series of simple checks to carry out before setting off for the sun. If you have any doubts or questions, please contact your Précisium garage on closer to you! Charge your vehicle correctly Make sure you do not exceed the total authorized load weight of your vehicle. It is indicated on the end of the doors or on your registration document. Excess weight affects both handling and braking, increasing the risk of an accident. Have your tires checked Tires are the car's only link to the road. The wear of the treads must therefore be checked using the wear indicators. This check will reduce the risk of losing control on oily-wet surfaces. If in doubt, have your tires examined by your Précisium repairer. Finally, before setting off, never forget to restore the correct pressures, including you will find the values ​​in the frame of the driver's door. Have your brakes checked A worn pad VERY loudly makes a deaf scraping noise when braking, it must be changed immediately. Make an appointment with your Précisium repairer quickly! Have your air conditioning checked You go out in the sun, so the temperature will normally be warmer. You have more, perhaps a certain number of kilometers to do, so do not neglect your air conditioning otherwise your trip may be uncomfortable.If you no longer feel enough cold air: have the gas charge checked refrigerant. Also remember to request the replacement of the cabin filter if necessary. Check your engine oil level Before each long journey, it is advisable to check the engine oil level. Take out the dipstick, wipe it dry, then return it to its location. Take it out again. The trace must be between the “mini” and “maxi” marks. In case of shortage, top up with an oil of the same viscosity. Check your coolant The level of your coolant should be checked on average once a month, engine cold! The level must be between the "mini" and "maxi" indicators on the expansion tank. Top up, if necessary, should only be done with coolant. Never use water, if in doubt, contact your Precisium repairer. Check your optics (headlights) To check and check your optics, it is important to listen to noises! Indeed, a turn signal that no longer works does not have the same noise anymore. For stop lights, it is a little more complicated . You have several solutions: Have someone stay behind your vehicle, and when stationary, press the brake pedal, if the lights do not come on, your bulbs no longer work. Back up against a wall and you whether or not you will clearly see the red lights. If you still have questions or have any doubts, contact your nearest Precisium!
