Imprint Terms and Conditions In your shopping cart: 0 items, 0.00 EUR 1979: Company foundation Historical development The love for automobiles and motorcycles began early on with Uli Egetemeir. As an active "crosser", he had to screw, tinker and improve himself. So inevitably there was an apprenticeship as a motor vehicle mechanic and years later a master craftsman examination in the motor vehicle trade. In 1979, Uli Egetemeir founded his company EGU in a shed in Fellbach-Oeffingen. 1980: First EGU tuning kit The H. Koch MO tester drives the Yamaha SR 600 with 40 hp. 1990: EGU racing team in 1st place With Hans-Peter Mayer as the driver, the EGU-Yamaha with a 660 engine usually took the top positions. In September 1990, Hansi Meyer took 1st place in the race at Daimler-Benz. The SOS team was formed by a group of Crailsheim inventors and Uli Egetemeir. The short name "SOS" of the class founded in 1988 in motorsport stands for "Sound of Singles". Kernig single-cylinder four-stroke engines of various makes were armed "with great effort and detail" and put into different frame types. 1993: "EGU-Kat-Control", Messe Frankfurt ... and in the same year the Baden-Württemberg Environmental Innovation Award for a regulated catalyst for injection and carburetor engines. 1994: Promotional award for handicrafts Awarded by the Baden-Württemberg Minister of Economic Affairs Martin Herzog for exemplary achievements for securing the future in handicrafts. 1995: BaWü Environmental Innovation Award Wankel-Raps motorcycle study 1999: EGU senior at the Mercedes plant in Stuttgart The former Mercedes racing mechanic Josef Egetemeir was a VIP guest in the racing team in 1999. 2001: 80 years of Moto Guzzi On the occasion of the 80th anniversary of Moto Guzzi, Uli Egetemeir visits the factory. 2005: EGU SR 600 successful on the Oberjoch At the memorial mountain race on October 15, 2005 on the Oberjoch, the EGU SR 600 with 50 HP and 600 ccm performed successfully in the time trial. 2006: EGU FMX 700 with the fastest time on the Oberjoch At the memorial mountain race on October 15, 2006 on the Oberjoch, Mr. Poschenrieder, European champion on the sand track, achieved the fastest time with the EGU FMX 700 with 59 HP and 700 ccm.
