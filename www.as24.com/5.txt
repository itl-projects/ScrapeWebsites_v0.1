The toll system is called PREMID, in Czech Dálniční poplatek or Myto. The toll is a government tax which concerns all motor vehicles with a total authorized laden weight of more than 3.5 tonnes. First class expressways, motorways and roads are subject to tolls. Your AS 24 toll solutions in the Czech Republic: how it works Applicable toll systems In the Czech Republic, tolls are fully automatic using the ON system Board Unit (OBU), which works by microwave like the PASSango AS 24 badge. The law obliges road users entering the Czech Republic to be in possession of a Pre- or Post-Pay badge. AS 24Pre-Pay solutions In the event that your trucks only run occasionally in the Czech Republic, it is preferable to choose the Pre-Pay mode. Each PREMID is associated with an AS 24 EUROTRAFIC card. Your badge can be credited with an amount between 60 and 580 euros, rechargeable at 100 GO.Post-Pay points of sale For more regular uses, AS 24 offers the Post-Pay badge to simplify the crossing of tolls. Product AS 24 EUROTRAFIC card Materialization Description The card which gives you access to the toll and station networks in Europe. It also facilitates the payment of foreign taxes. Operation at the toll station Gantry cranes are built on the Czech road network. They are subject to tolls and equipped with antennas ensuring communications with the PREMID On Board Unit. Customs control posts check that the trucks are properly equipped with the OBU (On Board Unit) system. Mobile stations can also be deployed. It is the driver who is legally responsible for the proper functioning of the OBU. As there is no toll station forcing the driver to stop or slow down, there is no lane to choose. On the other hand, the driver must absolutely make sure that the OBU (On Board Unit) has emitted an audible signal, confirming that its passage has been detected by the gantry.
