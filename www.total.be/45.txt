Check your spelling or do a new search. Engine oil What is engine oil? Home My vehicle Engine oils What is engine oil? The base oil can be: Mineral: that is to say obtained by distillation of certain oils ("crude oils") and extraction in a refinery, Synthetic technology: these are hydrocracked or hydro-isomerized bases obtained after deep conversion (catalytic cracking of molecules then recomposition and finishing) on ​​heavy petroleum fractions (hydrocracking), paraffins or naphthas (hydroisomerization). Vegetable: pine, rapeseed, peanut, palm, castor origin; they can be used for the formulation of biodegradable lubricants. Synthetic or synthetic bases have excellent physical properties and exceptional thermal stability. They increase the longevity of the engine, allow controlled consumption and longer drain intervals. Additives are present at 15% to 25% in finished oils. Either to strengthen certain properties of the base oil Or to give the base oil properties that it does not naturally have. There are many different additives, but 7 are of particular importance. These are antiwear, antioxidant, detergent, dispersant, anticorrosive, anti-foam and extreme pressure additives.
