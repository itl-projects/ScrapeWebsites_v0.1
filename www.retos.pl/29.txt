Renault Trucks believes that the future of urban transport is electricity, which will improve the quality of life in city centers and globally affect CO2 emissions. The effect of this approach is the signing of a contract with the Carlsberg Group for the supply of 20 Renault D Wide Z.E. to the Feldschlösschen brewery in Switzerland. Bruno Blin, president of Renault Trucks, said: This is the first order of this scale in Europe. The fact that these vehicles are delivered shows our customers that the transport sector is undergoing a major transformation and reflects the actual current needs of the industrial and transport industries: our 100% electric Renault Trucks vehicles that are coming off the assembly line and will be on the road in a few weeks are the answer Of Europe. " 20 Renault Trucks D Wide Z.E. vehicles The 26t ordered by Carlsberg will perform daily deliveries over a distance of 100 to 200 km to supply Swiss customers of the Feldschlösschen brewery with 15 logistics centers located in Switzerland. It is worth noting that Feldschlösschen is involved in zero-emission logistics, partly through production facilities served by railways. With a wide range of electric vehicles with a GVW of 3.1 to 26 tons, Renault Trucks meets both the current and future requirements of transport companies wishing to operate in cities. These vehicles are ideally suited to the needs of the municipal industry, enabling noiseless work at unconventional hours (for example, night delivery), while ensuring the improvement of the quality of life of local residents. Renault Trucks D and D Wide Z.E. vehicles are produced at the production plant in Blainville-sur-Orne (Calvados, France). Technical specification of Renault Trucks D Wide Z.E .: - DMC 26 tons.
