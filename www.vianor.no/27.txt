If it is warmer than +25 C or colder than 0 C, the properties of the rubber can change, which will shorten the tire's life. Storage in cool conditions best preserves the properties of the rubber product. Moisture Too humid storage conditions should be avoided. The moisture in the storage compartment should not be so high that there will be water on the tires. The tires should also not be stored so that they are exposed directly to rain or water. Light The tires should be protected from light, especially direct sunlight and light from strong lamps with high UV content. Acid and ozone Ozone gas has a strong negative impact on tires. There should be no ozone-emitting appliances in the tire storage room. Examples of such appliances are fluorine or mercury lamps, high voltage electrical appliances, electric motors or other electrical appliances which may cause sparks or discharges. pressure or twisting. Heavy deformation during prolonged storage can cause damage when the tires are put into service and are normally loaded.
