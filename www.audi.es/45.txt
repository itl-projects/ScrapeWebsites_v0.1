Since September 1, 2017, certain new vehicles have been homologated using the World Wide Harmonized Light Vehicle Test Procedure (WLTP), which is a more realistic new test procedure for measuring fuel consumption and CO2 emissions . Since September 1, 2018, the WLTP replaced the European NEDC driving cycle. Given the more realistic test conditions, fuel consumption and CO2 emissions measured under the WLTP are often higher than those measured under the NEDC. For more information on the differences between the WLTP and NEDC values, please visit our website www.audi.es/wltp In the case of vehicles homologated in accordance with the WLTP regulations, the NEDC values ​​would derive from the information obtained under said WLTP regulations. . Both values ​​(WLTP and NEDC) are exclusively for comparison between the different types of vehicles. These data do not refer to a specific vehicle and do not constitute part of the offer.
