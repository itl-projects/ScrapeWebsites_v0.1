High-pressure washing Technological precursor of the process, Eléphant Bleu introduced high-pressure car washing in France in 1973. It is only after several years of testing that the process is mastered and allows high-quality high-pressure washing without removing the paint. . The advantage of high pressure washing Unique by its quality, its precision, its limited and controlled water consumption, its simplicity, high pressure washing in one of our Blue Elephant washing centers is recognized for the performance of its value for money .High pressure is the freedom to choose your washing time, to insist on the dirtiest parts and to obtain a perfect wash with a minimum of water, without mechanical contact on the bodywork. Because water sprayed in a sprayed form has a powerful mechanical action while using a volume of water equivalent to that of a shower (40 to 50 l). On this simple principle, the technical process is complex and requires high precision to guarantee the best efficiency without risk for the bodywork. Creator of the car wash system and with more than 40 years of experience, Blue Elephant has perfect mastery of this technology, of which it offers the most successful version. Among the 6 stages proposed, it's up to you to compose the program that best suits your expectations and the dirtiness of your vehicle. To make the most of the Blue Elephant excellence, we advise you: to start with the “Rim Wash” option, available on your washing track, to progressively complete all washing steps. The level of equipment at our stations and our advice gives you the guarantee of a professional wash. Light and handy, the high pressure lances, which spray water with atomized jets, are exclusive to the brand. Washing at Blue Elephant is economical and ecological 80% water saving: A High Pressure washing at Blue Elephant is 40 to 50 l of water consumed on average per wash, up to 6 times less water on average than a home wash which can consume up to 300 l of water! 100% of treated waste: The hydrocarbons are recovered in a separator and then treated, the other residues are collected then destroyed or revalorized . The water thus decontaminated is then routed through conventional wastewater networks. Did you know ? The residual water from washing contains many polluting substances: Hydrocarbons which have significant toxic effects on flora and fauna Metallic pollution: heavy metals (lead or cadmium) which are concentrated in living organisms such as fish and thus penetrate into the food chain Phosphates which cause proliferation of algae preventing bathing On the basis of a frequency of 7 washes / year, Blue Elephant estimates at 95,000 tonnes, the amount of pollutants contained in the washing water, or what pollute 95 million m3, the equivalent of the annual consumption of drinking water in households in an agglomeration such as Lyon or Marseille ...
